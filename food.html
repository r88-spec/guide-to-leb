Lebanese cuisine is a delightful fusion of fresh ingredients, aromatic spices, and centuries-old traditions. From savory mezze like hummus, tabbouleh, and baba ghanoush to hearty dishes like kibbeh and falafel, every meal is a celebration of flavors. Whether enjoyed in a bustling Beirut restaurant or a cozy village home, Lebanese food offers a rich, vibrant experience that reflects the countrys diverse culture and warm hospitality.

MOST FAMOUS DISHES IN LEBANON?

•MASHEWI

Lebanese *mashewi* refers to a variety of grilled dishes that are central to Lebanese cuisine. Typically prepared over an open flame, *mashewi* includes tender marinated meats like lamb, chicken, and beef, often skewered as *kebabs*. These are seasoned with a mix of Middle Eastern spices, such as sumac, cumin, and allspice, and served alongside fresh vegetables, garlic sauce (*toum*), and flatbreads. A staple at gatherings and feasts, *mashewi* embodies the Lebanese love for communal dining and bold flavors.

•SHAWARMA

Lebanese shawarma is a beloved street food featuring thinly sliced, marinated meat—usually chicken, beef, or lamb—slow-cooked on a vertical rotisserie. The meat is seasoned with a blend of Middle Eastern spices, including cinnamon, paprika, and cardamom, creating its signature flavor. Served in a warm pita wrap, shawarma is typically paired with garlic sauce (*toum*), tahini, pickles, and fresh vegetables. It's a flavorful and satisfying dish that embodies the rich culinary heritage of Lebanon.

•HUMMUS

Hummus is a classic Lebanese dip made from blended chickpeas, tahini (sesame paste), lemon juice, garlic, and olive oil. Creamy and rich in flavor, it is a staple of Lebanese cuisine and a popular dish throughout Lebanon. Typically served as part of a mezze (appetizer spread), hummus is enjoyed with warm pita bread or fresh vegetables. Its simplicity, versatility, and nutritional benefits make it a beloved dish in Lebanese households and restaurants.

•TABOULEH

Tabbouleh is a traditional Lebanese salad made with finely chopped parsley, tomatoes, onions, and mint, mixed with bulgur wheat and dressed in olive oil, lemon juice, and a touch of salt. Light, refreshing, and bursting with flavor, it is a staple of Lebanese cuisine and a favorite at gatherings. Often served as part of a mezze (appetizer spread), tabbouleh highlights the fresh, vibrant ingredients central to Lebanese cooking.
